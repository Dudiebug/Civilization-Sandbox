# AI Decision Laboratory

## Purpose
Prove the common decision runtime before broad person, settlement, or government behavior is built. The laboratory exists to prevent inconsistent AI frameworks and hidden authority/knowledge errors; it is not a requirement to build a universal simulation platform before Version 1.0.

## Required Version 1.0 capabilities
- Versioned behavior-contract catalog with stable IDs.
- Deterministic scenario fixture loader and headless runner.
- Candidate, precondition, observation, score, selection, and blocker trace capture.
- Positive, negative, boundary, save/replay, and bounded performance tests.
- Property or metamorphic checks where the contract has an important invariant or directional claim.
- Before/after trace comparison.
- Deliberate-defect tests sufficient to prove each implemented primitive family can fail visibly.

## First three decisions
1. Founding-site selection.
2. Urgent personal-needs action.
3. Settlement labor allocation.

## Deferred laboratory breadth
A broad statistical dashboard, large contract catalog, full retail trace UX, and Version 1.5-scale batch evaluation are not required before the first three decisions and the selected Version 1.0 scale tier pass.

## Gate
Do not proceed to broad autonomous behavior until the three decisions pass their declared deterministic scenarios, invariants, save/replay, trace, and measured budget gates. The exact seed counts, defect count, and scale tier are set in the approved Milestone 0.2 plan rather than hard-coded now.

Failure means redesign primitives and fixtures, not merely tune more weights.
