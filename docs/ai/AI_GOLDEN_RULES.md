# AI Golden Rules

1. **Authority before utility.** An actor may evaluate only decisions owned by its layer.
2. **Knowledge before scoring.** Observations are filtered by the actor's beliefs, confidence, and data age.
3. **Feasibility before preference.** Missing law, route, material, skill, or capacity remains a blocker.
4. **Bounded work.** Every candidate set, spatial query, observation set, and plan template has a hard cap.
5. **Deterministic diversity.** Keyed variation is reproducible and cannot bypass preconditions.
6. **Commitments matter.** Hysteresis, cooldown, invalidation, and sunk cost prevent constant thrashing.
7. **Reservations are real.** Unique goods, sites, labor commitments, and authority cannot be double-spent.
8. **Explain while deciding.** Losing candidates and rejected preconditions are captured when they still exist.
9. **No camera effect.** Visible, selected, or observed status never changes authoritative choice.
10. **Regression is a behavior defect.** Every confirmed defect becomes a fixture.
