# Version 1.0 Identity Guardrails

These guardrails define what may not be cut while reducing scope. A smaller release can reduce breadth, counts, fidelity, and system depth; it must not become a generic colony builder or passive simulation viewer.

## Required identity
1. **Persistent named people** - individuals have stable identity, location, household/work role, needs, life state, and participation in major events.
2. **Physical causality** - settlements grow through located resources, labor, hauling, projects, buildings, routes, damage, and rebuilding.
3. **Societal divergence** - at least two societies can make visibly different choices because their conditions, institutions, or histories differ.
4. **Autonomous exact implementation** - the player directs objectives and priorities; simulated actors choose ordinary sites, routes, staffing, and execution.
5. **Omniscient inspectability** - the player can inspect authoritative state and obtain a useful plain-language reason for major outcomes.
6. **Layered god-player agency** - Binding Orders, indirect interventions, and Force Outcomes remain distinct and auditable.
7. **Durable consequences** - major events and physical scars remain linked to later state and survive save/load.

## Minimum Version 1.0 expression
- A named person can be followed from need to work, movement, construction, migration, injury, or death.
- A settlement can be seen growing from real deliveries and labor.
- A route or building can be damaged, causing shortages or displacement, and later repaired or replaced.
- Two societies can respond differently to the same class of pressure.
- A player can observe, issue a constrained order, alter a condition, and force an outcome, then inspect the difference.
- A timeline or history view can connect the resulting major events and ruins.

## Safe reductions
- Smaller world and population.
- Fewer resources, buildings, occupations, technologies, government models, powers, and UI screens.
- Broader commodity batches and simplified markets.
- Simplified households, politics, diplomacy, combat, ecology, and history.
- Shorter release soak and history horizon when accepted from measured evidence.

## Unsafe reductions
- Abstract settlement upgrades that appear without physical projects.
- Scripted society personalities standing in for causal divergence.
- Ordinary manual placement replacing autonomous implementation.
- Hidden AI decisions that cannot explain major outcomes.
- Force powers that bypass data integrity or history.
- Off-screen fabricated outcomes or camera-dependent simulation.

Every milestone and release gate includes an identity review against this file.
