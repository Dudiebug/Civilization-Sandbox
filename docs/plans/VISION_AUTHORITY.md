# Vision Authority and Preservation Contract

The delivery plan may change. The game vision may not be silently reduced.

## Immutable north-star identity

Civilization Sandbox is an endless, real-time, physically represented civilization sandbox. Persistent fictional people autonomously build settlements and societies, develop different responses to geography and history, interact through material systems, and leave inspectable histories and physical scars. The player is an unseen omniscient force who may observe, issue binding direction, alter conditions, or force outcomes.

The authoritative Blueprint v2.0 remains included unchanged in this kit. This roadmap changes sequencing and task granularity only.

## Required identity through every release

- Persistent named people with stable identity and consequential lives.
- Physical causality: resources, labor, hauling, projects, buildings, routes, damage, rebuilding, ruins, and scars exist in the world.
- Societal divergence caused by conditions, institutions, knowledge, resources, accidents, and history rather than scripted faction personalities.
- Autonomous exact implementation: actors choose ordinary sites, routes, staffing, and execution while the player directs higher-level intent.
- Omniscient inspectability with useful reasons for important outcomes.
- Separate Binding Orders, indirect interventions, and Force Outcomes.
- Durable events and physical consequences that survive save/load and influence later history.
- Deterministic, camera-independent authoritative simulation with stable identity and semantic persistence.
- Coded, bounded, explainable intelligence rather than live-LLM citizens or a black-box civilization brain.

## Full scope preserved

The roadmap preserves:

- Lean regional Version 1.0 as the first complete game.
- Complete early-modern breadth as Version 1.5.
- Industrial, electrical, mechanized, atomic/information, divergent-future, orbital, and multi-planet possibility spaces after Version 1.5.
- The full people, household, settlement, economy, logistics, knowledge, culture, religion, politics, diplomacy, warfare, ecosystem, history, storyteller, player-power, interface, persistence, performance, accessibility, and production vision described by the Blueprint.

## Allowed changes

The creator may change counts, depth, order, presentation, tuning, release allocation, or implementation after observing the game. A feature may move between releases without being erased from the north-star backlog.

## Prohibited silent changes

An AI agent may not:

- delete a vision item because it is difficult;
- convert physical causality into unexplained abstract upgrades;
- replace autonomous execution with ordinary manual city placement;
- make authoritative outcomes camera-dependent or fabricated off-screen;
- merge Binding Orders and Force Outcomes into one undifferentiated command path;
- treat a deferred feature as cancelled;
- rewrite the Blueprint or this preservation contract without explicit creator approval.

## Traceability rule

Every major Blueprint system must appear in `SCOPE_COVERAGE.md` as one of:

- introduced in a playable build;
- deepened in Version 1.1-1.5;
- reserved for a named later-age release;
- explicitly classified as never-core by the Blueprint.

Unmapped scope is a planning defect.
