# Milestone 0.4 - Physical Settlement

**Status:** Planned; task contracts intentionally deferred until Milestone 0.3 passes.
**Detailed program context:** `PRE_1_0_ROADMAP.md`, Section 8.

## Purpose
Turn the Living Camp into a durable autonomous settlement through a bounded physical economy. This milestone proves settlement continuity and recovery before multiplying society models.

## Entry conditions
- Milestone 0.3 gate is approved.
- Living Camp performance, save growth, path/query costs, and creator-playtest findings are available.
- D05 is opened and resolved only to the depth required for this milestone.

## Player-visible result
Homes, storage, production, services, routes, and public/shared projects appear because people obtain inputs, haul them, and complete authoritative work. Damage causes real capacity loss, displacement, salvage, repair, and rebuilding pressure.

## Workstreams
1. Approved resource/inventory envelope with conservation, location, reservations, and declared sources/sinks.
2. Gathering, farming/extraction, bounded recipes, workplaces, occupations, tools, and labor demand.
3. Approved building archetypes and complete project lifecycle from proposal to occupation, cancellation, damage, repair, demolition, and salvage.
4. Settlement project selection, minimum parcel/right representation, and reachable services.
5. Path formalization, road capacity/condition, and one representative bridge/corridor bottleneck.
6. Settlement inspector and a small readable set of resource, work, construction, service, and route overlays.
7. Multi-year continuation, save/load, replay, performance, and trace-volume control.

## Integrated gate scenario
A camp becomes the approved durable settlement form through physical deliveries and labor. A critical route or neighborhood is damaged. Shelter, production, services, access, and priorities change. The settlement repairs, reroutes, adapts, or declines for traceable reasons. Save/load preserves active projects, damage, displacement, and recovery commitments.

## Required gate evidence
- resource and inventory conservation;
- labor/workplace and project candidate budgets;
- construction attribution and cancellation behavior;
- reachable-service and route-bottleneck tests;
- damage, displacement, salvage, and rebuilding scenario;
- deterministic replay and semantic save continuity;
- performance/save-growth report;
- independent review and creator acceptance.

## Decisions opened
- D05 resource, building, occupation, and settlement-depth envelope;
- one representative settlement Binding Order used to verify decision-level control.

## Decisions intentionally left open
Final content counts, multiple society/government models, broad markets/credit, inter-settlement trade, religion/factions, warfare, complete powers, city breadth, and release content lock.

## Stop or redesign triggers
- Settlement growth is primarily a threshold or abstract bonus rather than visible work.
- Project/site searches become unbounded.
- Production or services depend on teleported goods/labor.
- Recovery requires deleting damage/history or manual normal-city placement.
- The approved scale tier cannot continue within the 0.4 budget.

## Handoff to 0.5
Deliver stable settlement primitives, measured economy/infrastructure behavior, damage/recovery continuity, and a neutral/reference policy configuration suitable for matched divergence experiments.
