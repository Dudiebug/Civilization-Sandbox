# Version 1.0 Production Coverage - Founding Worlds

This is the lean regional release contract. Exact quantities are recorded in the Milestone 0.9 release-scope manifest rather than fixed prematurely here.

## Identity
- [ ] Persistent named people remain inspectable through life, work, movement, migration, injury, and death.
- [ ] Physical resources, projects, buildings, routes, damage, salvage, rebuilding, and ruins drive outcomes.
- [ ] Multiple societies demonstrate visible, explainable divergence.
- [ ] Ordinary exact implementation remains autonomous.
- [ ] Binding Orders, indirect interventions, and Force Outcomes are distinct and auditable.
- [ ] Major history and physical scars survive save/load and later consequences.

## Regional world and life
- [ ] One approved regional world envelope with enough environmental/resource contrast for the release loop.
- [ ] The approved starting and mature population tiers run within budget.
- [ ] Lightweight households, needs, health/life state, occupations/work, and migration support the implemented loop.

## Settlement and economy
- [ ] A camp can become the approved durable settlement form through real labor, hauling, and construction.
- [ ] A bounded resource/production/stockpile model creates meaningful shortages and choices.
- [ ] Routes and at least one critical bottleneck affect access, trade, movement, and settlement response.
- [ ] Damage causes real capacity loss, displacement, salvage, and rebuilding pressure.

## Society and interaction
- [ ] The approved society contrast model produces measurable divergence without scripted faction identities.
- [ ] Contact, physical exchange, route dependence, and migration connect societies.
- [ ] Limited diplomacy, conflict, peace, and recovery are coherent at the approved depth.

## Player and information
- [ ] Omniscient search/inspection exposes all implemented authoritative systems.
- [ ] Major decisions and failures have useful plain-language explanations linked to evidence.
- [ ] The approved Version 1.0 powers cover each player-control class.
- [ ] The UI consolidates all required capabilities accessibly; no fixed workspace count is required.

## Persistence and quality
- [ ] Stable IDs, deterministic replay, semantic saves, migrations, atomic recovery, and diagnostics pass.
- [ ] The approved tick, memory, presentation, save/load, accelerated-run, and soak budgets pass on reference hardware.
- [ ] Major events, interventions, and protected ruins remain factual and bounded.
- [ ] Accessibility baseline, onboarding, release packaging, known limitations, and creator acceptance pass.

## Explicit non-blockers for Version 1.0
The complete 10,000-person scale, all large presets, 18 resources, 16 buildings, 16 occupations, six institutions, four government presets, 12 cultural tendencies, six technology domains, 12 diplomacy actions, 24 powers, 13 workspaces, full ecosystems, Story Director profiles, historical maps, and 500-year release gate are Version 1.5 horizon items unless explicitly promoted through a creator-approved scope decision.
