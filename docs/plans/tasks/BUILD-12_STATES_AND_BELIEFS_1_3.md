# Prompt - Build 12: States and Beliefs Version 1.3

Deepen political, institutional, religious, cultural, and technological identity without turning societies into scripted factions. Preserve the Blueprint's emergent, physical, inspectable, deterministic simulation principles.

Player outcome: societies form recognizably different governments, laws, institutions, beliefs, loyalties, and capability paths; those structures can reform, fail, split, or be replaced because of people, material conditions, legitimacy, and history.

Inspect the current build and propose 3-8 playable vertical slices. Implement one approved slice at a time. Candidate slices include government presets, office and succession, law and enforcement, legitimacy, factions, reform, coups or rebellion, religion and ritual, patriotism and assimilation, capability prerequisites, invention, diffusion, loss, rediscovery, and reverse engineering. Connect each slice to named people, collective decisions, material capacity, visible institutions, causal explanation, saves, and tests.

Do not implement a universal linear technology tree or arbitrary ideology meters detached from people and institutions. Capability should depend on knowledge, tools, organizations, materials, and transmission. Political changes must have observable supporters, opposition, authority, costs, and consequences.

Acceptance: comparable societies can develop different stable or unstable arrangements; succession or reform can succeed or fail for inspectable reasons; a capability can spread, be lost, or be rediscovered; old saves migrate; accelerated runs remain deterministic; and presentation never writes authoritative state directly.

Keep every unimplemented Blueprint institution, belief, and technology requirement visible in the scope matrix for Version 1.5 closure.
