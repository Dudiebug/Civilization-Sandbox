# Prompt - Build 15: Industrial Transition Version 2.0

Extend the accepted early-modern game into an industrial transition that emerges from existing resources, knowledge, institutions, labor, infrastructure, and political conflict rather than appearing as a scripted era unlock.

Player outcome: societies industrialize along different paths through steam power, machine tools, coal, factories, rail, dense cities, mass education, labor organization, pollution, and industrial risk, while non-industrial alternatives remain possible.

Inspect the Blueprint's later-age direction, current simulation limits, and player feedback. Propose 3-8 playable slices and implement one approved slice at a time. Suggested sequence: enabling capability chain; steam and machine tools; factory production and wage labor; coal extraction and transport; rail networks; industrial urban growth and utilities; pollution/accidents; mass education and labor politics. Integrate every slice through people, materials, construction, logistics, institutions, visuals, history, persistence, and performance.

Industrialization must not be a universal linear tech tier. Factories require real inputs, workers, machines, transport, maintenance, knowledge, and demand. Pollution and accidents must have physical, health, political, and historical consequences.

Acceptance: at least two societies industrialize differently or one rejects/stalls for causal reasons; rail and factory systems materially reshape settlement networks; disruptions propagate physically; old worlds can continue; long-run determinism and performance are measured; and the build remains playable after each slice.

Defer electrification-specific grids and later-age systems to Version 3.0 unless the current industrial slice strictly requires a small enabling abstraction.
