# Prompt - Build 20: Distant Expansion

Extend civilization beyond the core world while preserving the same simulation identity: named continuity, physical logistics, autonomous societies, incomplete information, divergence, player authority distinctions, and factual history.

Player outcome: societies build orbital and interplanetary infrastructure, establish remote settlements, move people and material across real routes and delays, diverge under new conditions, and remain historically connected to their world of origin.

Begin with one bounded orbital playable slice, not a whole solar system. Inspect scale, performance, save, and authority constraints; then propose 3-8 playable slices. Candidate slices include orbital topology, launch industry, celestial resources, stations, transport schedules, communication delay, remote settlement founding, life-support logistics, scale transitions, autonomy, multi-planet governance, and cross-world history. Implement one approved slice at a time and keep the existing planetary game fully playable.

No teleporting people or goods, universal instant communication, or separate minigame that abandons the core simulation. Aggregation may reduce cost only when conservation, identity, causality, and replay remain provable. A named traveler, shipment, decision, and historical event must survive transitions between simulation scales.

Acceptance: a continuing world can found and sustain at least one remote settlement; delays and logistics create meaningful divergence; failure and rescue are causal; save/load and replay preserve cross-world identity; performance is measured; and the creator accepts each scale expansion before further breadth.

Expand to additional celestial bodies only through later creator-approved playable slices. The Blueprint remains the authority when technical convenience conflicts with the vision.
