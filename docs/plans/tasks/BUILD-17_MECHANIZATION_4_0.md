# Prompt - Build 17: Mechanization Version 4.0

Transform regional scale through petroleum logistics, automobiles, highways, aircraft, mechanized production, and mechanized conflict while retaining physical accounting and named human consequences.

Player outcome: faster transport changes cities, trade, migration, diplomacy, and warfare, but creates new dependencies on fuel, vehicles, roads, maintenance, industry, and specialized knowledge.

Inspect the electrified build and propose 3-8 playable slices. Implement one approved slice at a time. Candidate slices include petroleum extraction/refining, vehicle manufacture and ownership, road/highway networks, civilian freight, aviation infrastructure, air travel, mechanized military formations, fuel-based supply, tactical abstraction changes, suburban or corridor urban forms, and transport accidents/pollution.

Vehicles and aircraft must be produced, fueled, crewed, routed, maintained, damaged, and replaced. Faster simulation abstractions may aggregate movement but may not duplicate goods, teleport forces, erase casualties, or depend on camera visibility.

Acceptance: mechanization changes the economic geography of at least one continuing world; fuel or infrastructure disruption produces a traceable regional crisis; warfare reflects vehicle and supply constraints; save/replay/history remain coherent; and approved scale performance is measured.

Defer nuclear systems, digital computing, electronic surveillance, and strategic missiles to Version 5.0.
