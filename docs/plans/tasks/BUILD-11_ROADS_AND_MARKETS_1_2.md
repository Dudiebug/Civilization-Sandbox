# Prompt - Build 11: Roads and Markets Version 1.2

Expand the released game into a richer physical economy whose settlements develop distinct forms through resources, labor, ownership, trade, transport, and history. Preserve named continuity, deterministic authority, conservation of goods, and all accepted earlier behavior.

Player outcome: settlements visibly specialize, exchange a broader range of goods, build and maintain different route networks and workplaces, suffer understandable bottlenecks, and redevelop rather than growing as identical blobs.

Inspect the current economy, profiler data, Blueprint catalogs, and scope matrix. Propose 3-8 playable slices, then implement one approved slice at a time. Candidate slices include a bounded commodity/recipe chain, new occupations and services, ownership regimes, market prices and credit, workplace expansion, road and bridge hierarchies, shipment capacity, infrastructure maintenance, urban form, and redevelopment. Each slice must connect production, transport, decisions, visuals, explanation, persistence, and failure recovery.

Maintain physical accounting: no duplicated goods, instant transport, or camera-dependent simulation. Prices and credit may guide allocation but may not replace inventories, labor, travel, or delivery. Measure routefinding, shipment, and economy cost at the intended scale.

Acceptance: at least two settlements develop visibly different economic and spatial forms for causal reasons; a route or production disruption propagates through inventories, prices, work, construction, and recovery; save/load preserves the chain; and each slice leaves a fun runnable Unity build.

Do not attempt the entire Blueprint catalog in one change. Preserve unimplemented entries for Version 1.5 completion and document substitutions or count changes for creator approval.
