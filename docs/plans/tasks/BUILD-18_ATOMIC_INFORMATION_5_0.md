# Prompt - Build 18: Atomic and Information Age Version 5.0

Add atomic energy and weapons, missiles, computing, electronic networks, intelligence, surveillance, early automation, and deterrence as systems with long-lived material, political, social, and historical consequences.

Player outcome: societies face strategic choices involving energy, information, secrecy, automation, catastrophic risk, and deterrence; consequences persist across generations and reshape international behavior.

Inspect the mechanized world and propose 3-8 playable slices. Implement one approved slice at a time. Candidate slices include nuclear knowledge/material chains, reactors and radiation, weapons and delivery systems, deterrence and crisis diplomacy, computing institutions, electronic communication networks, intelligence and counterintelligence, surveillance and civil response, automation foundations, accidents, contamination, and long-term stewardship.

Strategic systems require real materials, facilities, expertise, command structures, information limits, maintenance, and risk. Do not give societies perfect knowledge. Do not resolve deterrence or intelligence through opaque random rolls without inspectable inputs and recorded outcomes. Maintain responsible, non-graphic presentation of mass harm.

Acceptance: at least one strategic crisis can escalate or de-escalate for causal reasons; accidents and radiation persist physically and historically; computing improves specific capacities rather than acting as magic; old worlds migrate; replay and save continuity hold; and the creator accepts the tone and authority boundaries.

Defer speculative biotechnology, synthetic environments, and other divergent futures to Version 6.0+.
