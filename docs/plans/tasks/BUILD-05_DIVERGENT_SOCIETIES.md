# Prompt - Build 05: Divergent Societies

Add multiple societies that develop visibly different solutions because of geography, resources, institutions, knowledge, leadership, and history rather than scripted faction identities.

Player outcome: I can run two matched or comparable societies, inspect their priorities and constraints, see different settlement forms or problem solutions emerge, and understand why their choices differed.

Begin with a paired-world experiment. Select the smallest useful society identity variables, institution/government layer, cultural or religious tendencies, knowledge/capability states, invention/adoption mechanism, and collective decision needed to demonstrate causal divergence. Plan 4-8 end-to-end slices and keep both societies playable after each.

Decisions must have an authority owner, permitted knowledge, bounded candidates, hard preconditions, deterministic tie-breaking, and a player-readable reason. Do not use moral scores, inherited ideology, random unlocks, or fixed national personalities.

Acceptance: controlled scenarios produce measurably different feasible outcomes linked to conditions and history; identical conditions do not diverge because of uncontrolled randomness; explanations expose decisive causes without pretending actors know hidden information.

Do not implement the complete government, culture, religion, or technology breadth reserved for Versions 1.3-1.5.
