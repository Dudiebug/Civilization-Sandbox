# Prompt - Build 13: War and World Version 1.4

Bring international politics, warfare, environment, god powers, and historical products to mature early-modern depth while preserving the game's named-person and physical-logistics identity.

Player outcome: a regional world conducts diplomacy, coercion, alliance, war, occupation, peace, ecological change, disaster response, and recovery, while producing useful chronicles and maps that explain what actually happened.

Inspect the existing systems and propose 3-8 playable slices. Implement one approved slice at a time. Candidate slices include additional diplomacy actions and treaties, alliance obligations, coercion, military organization and supply, occupation, ecosystems and land use, pollution, disasters, extinction/recovery, Story Director profiles, chronicles, historical maps, expanded powers, and history workspaces. Every slice must include player-visible outcomes, causal traces, persistence, performance measurements, and adversarial cases.

Combat and disasters must remain bounded physical processes with real casualties, displacement, damage, supply, and aftermath. The Story Director may select and frame real events but must never fabricate authoritative events. God powers must declare whether they observe, order, materially intervene, or force an outcome, and history must record the intervention.

Acceptance: a complete regional scenario combines diplomacy, conflict, environmental pressure, intervention, and recovery without losing people or goods; the chronicle and maps match authoritative history; replay/save continuity holds; and each slice remains playable.

Do not silently substitute spectacle for simulation. Preserve unfinished diplomacy actions, powers, workspaces, catalogs, and history products for the Version 1.5 completion gate.
