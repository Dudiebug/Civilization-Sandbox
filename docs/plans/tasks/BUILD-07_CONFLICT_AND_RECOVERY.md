# Prompt - Build 07: Conflict and Recovery

Add limited physical conflict whose consequences remain attached to people, supplies, settlements, politics, and history.

Player outcome: a dispute can produce war goals, mobilization, supplied combat groups, physical encounters, casualties, flight, building/route damage, occupation at the approved minimum depth, peace, demobilization, salvage, rebuilding, and persistent ruins.

Choose the smallest conflict model that creates meaningful human and physical consequences. Plan 4-8 vertical slices and preserve playability after each. Prefer bounded sector encounters and persistent people over per-projectile authoritative physics or tactical micromanagement.

Combat cannot fabricate supply, casualties, or off-screen outcomes. Participants, locations, causes, losses, displacement, peace terms, and recovery work must be factual and save-compatible. Explain decisions using actors' believed information.

Acceptance: one integrated scenario proceeds from tension through conflict, damage, peace, displacement, and recovery; individuals and supplies remain coherent; the same command log replays deterministically; scars and major events remain inspectable.

Do not add modern warfare, detailed navies, insurgency breadth, strategic deterrence, realistic gore, or the complete Version 1.5 doctrine/content set.
