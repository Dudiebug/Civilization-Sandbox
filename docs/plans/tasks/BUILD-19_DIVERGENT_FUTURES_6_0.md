# Prompt - Build 19: Divergent Futures Version 6.0+

Create multiple genuinely different future trajectories without imposing one universal endgame. Extend only paths consistent with the Blueprint's causal, physical, named, inspectable civilization simulation.

Player outcome: civilizations pursue and combine distinct futures in automation, biotechnology, energy, synthetic environments, governance, and social organization, producing benefits, dependencies, conflict, inequality, failure, and recovery unique to their histories.

Before implementation, use current-world evidence, creator priorities, and the preserved Blueprint direction to select one future family. Propose 3-8 playable slices for that family and implement one approved slice at a time. Possible families include deep automation, engineered biology, alternate high-density energy, synthetic habitats, institutional futures, ecological restoration, or other creator-approved paths. Add another family only after the first is playable, measured, and integrated.

Every future capability must have prerequisites, material infrastructure, expertise, ownership or governance, maintenance, failure modes, distributional effects, visible consequences, causal explanations, persistence, and history. Avoid black-box authoritative ML, magic post-scarcity, forced convergence, and content that makes earlier systems irrelevant.

Acceptance: two societies can reach meaningfully different viable futures; their paths remain legible from prior geography, institutions, capabilities, and choices; failures have physical consequences and recovery paths; old worlds remain playable; and each added family passes creator vision review.

Do not begin orbital or multi-planet expansion until a stable future-era world demonstrates that existing named continuity, logistics, autonomy, and history can survive the added scale.
