# Prompt - Build 06: Connected Region

Connect settlements through contact, physical exchange, route capacity, agreements, knowledge movement, and migration.

Player outcome: settlements discover one another, form beliefs from available information, send real shipments, depend on roads/bridges/corridors, make simple agreements, respond to shortage, and gain or lose people through migration.

Choose a bounded trade/allocation model from current play needs. Plan 4-8 playable slices: contact, shipment manifests, route capacity/delay, exchange, one or two agreement types, migration response, and regional overlays/explanations. Keep prices or money simple unless play demonstrates they are necessary.

Shipments must conserve goods and occupy real routes over time. Actors use believed rather than omniscient information. Queries and route work must remain bounded. Save/reload must preserve commitments, cargo, agreements, and migration decisions.

Acceptance: destroying or blocking one critical corridor produces a visible causal chain through delayed/lost shipments, shortage, policy or allocation response, and migration; restoring or replacing the route changes the outcome without duplicating goods.

Do not add the full diplomacy action set, complex finance, large trade networks, warfare, occupation, or final regional UI.
