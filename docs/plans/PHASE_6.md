# Milestone 0.7 - Conflict and Recovery

**Status:** Planned; task contracts intentionally deferred until Milestone 0.6 passes.
**Detailed program context:** `PRE_1_0_ROADMAP.md`, Section 11.

## Purpose
Add limited physical conflict only after civilian settlement, supply, routes, and migration function reliably.

## Entry conditions
- Connected Region gate passes.
- Persistent people and settlements can absorb labor loss, displacement, route damage, and rebuilding through ordinary systems.
- D08 is resolved from tone, readability, and performance prototypes.

## Player-visible result
Mobilization removes named people from ordinary life, consumes supplies, moves along routes, causes bounded physical encounters, injuries, deaths, damage, flight, peace, salvage, and rebuilding. Consequences persist after fighting ends.

## Workstreams
1. Approved militia/guard or small combat-group recruitment and command authority.
2. Broad equipment, ammunition/supply, readiness, and route dependence.
3. Bounded operation goals and strategic/local movement.
4. Group-level authoritative encounter resolution with outcomes allocated to persistent people.
5. Stylized directionally readable presentation and accessibility controls.
6. Civilian shelter, flight, displacement, temporary housing, return, and labor/knowledge loss.
7. Limited control/occupation only if approved, followed by peace, demobilization, salvage, repair, and rebuilding.
8. Conflict goals, causes, casualties, displacement, damage, and peace history.

## Integrated gate scenario
A traceable pressure creates an approved conflict. People mobilize, supplies move, an encounter occurs, civilians respond, named casualties and physical damage persist, and official peace/withdrawal is followed by recovery attempts. Peace cannot restore resources, people, infrastructure, or trust automatically.

## Required gate evidence
- authority/goal and believed-information tests;
- mobilization labor/household effects;
- supply/readiness and route dependence;
- deterministic encounter replay and casualty accounting;
- displacement/shelter continuity;
- damage, peace, salvage, and rebuilding behavior;
- presentation/accessibility and active-sector performance;
- independent review and creator acceptance.

## Decisions opened
- D08 conflict scale, violence presentation, occupation depth, and recovery boundary.

## Decisions intentionally left open
Detailed military hierarchy, projectile physics, artillery/naval/air breadth, deep occupation/insurgency, prisoners at depth, complex treaty catalog, later-era weapons, and final combat content counts.

## Stop or redesign triggers
- On-screen and off-screen authority differs.
- Combat bypasses supply, persistent people, or physical location.
- Casualties or refugees are fabricated/deleted outside declared events.
- Presentation alters authoritative outcomes or is unreadable with billboards.
- Recovery requires special scripted resets.

## Handoff to 0.8
Deliver a complete regional causal simulation over which the player can exercise observation, decision-level direction, indirect intervention, and explicit force.
