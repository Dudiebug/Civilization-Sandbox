# Game Vision

## One sentence
An endless real-time civilization sandbox where persistent fictional people physically build societies, diverge through geography and history, trade and fight across generations, and can be fully inspected, directed, damaged, restored, or forcibly reshaped by an unseen omniscient player.

## Release boundaries
- **Version 1.0:** a complete regional expression of the game's identity and causal loop.
- **Versions 1.1-1.4:** staged early-modern depth releases selected at later gates.
- **Version 1.5:** the complete fictional early-modern / roughly 1700s-equivalent game originally labeled Version 1.0 in Blueprint v2.0.
- **Version 2.0+:** industrial and later possibility spaces.

Version 1.0 must stand alone as a coherent game. It does not need the full content and scale breadth of Version 1.5.

## Design pillars
1. Persistent people and camera-independent causality.
2. Physical historical continuity: construction, damage, roads, ruins, and scars exist in the world.
3. Civilizations solve similar problems differently.
4. Unrestricted player freedom with separate Binding Order and Force Outcome paths.
5. Omniscience without information overload.
6. Society-dependent urban form without ordinary manual placement.
7. Explainable coded intelligence.
8. Extinction, recovery, and persistent landmarks as later depth where not required for the first regional release.
9. Verification, performance, and migration are product features.

## Version 1.0 player promise
The player can watch named people create settlements, inspect why important outcomes occurred, compare societies, influence decision-level priorities, alter conditions, use explicit Force Outcomes, and revisit durable consequences. There is no mandatory victory condition.

## Scope rule
Scale down breadth, counts, and subsystem depth before cutting identity. Exact quantitative targets are approved at milestone gates rather than embedded prematurely in this vision.
